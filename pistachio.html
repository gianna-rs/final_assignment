Pistachio Bark Cookies

Makes approx. 24 cookies

The only kind of chocolate bark I've ever liked is the white chocolate bark with pistachios and cranberries. So why not make my favourite bark into a great sugar cookie!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 egg, room temperature
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup white chocolate, chopped pieces or chips
  • 3/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup pistachios, salted and shelled

Instructions

  1. In a mixer or with a hand mixer, beat softened butter until smooth. Add both sugars and mix on medium speed until light in colour. Add egg and vanilla and beat on high speed until well incorporated.
  2. Mix all dry ingredients together in a separate bowl. Slowly mix dry ingredients into wet ingredients on low speed until just combined. Add chocolate, cranberries and pistachios - dough will be very thick and chunky!
  3. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or up to 2 days. You have to chill this dough otherwise your cookies will spread and melt in the oven!
  4. After chilling, let your dough sit at room temperature for 10-20 minutes or until slightly softened - it'll be easier to work with!
  5. Preheat your oven to 350° and prepare 2 large baking sheets with parchment paper or baking mats.
  6. Scoop 1 tbsp size balls of dough onto your prepared baking sheets.
  7. Bake 5-10 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden brown - the cookies will still look really soft!
  8. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 more minutes before moving them to a cooling rack. Once completely cool, take a bite and enjoy!
